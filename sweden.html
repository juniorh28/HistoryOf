History of: Sweden

Sweden is a powerful nation that is full of rich culture, magnificent landscape, and strong historical influence. The country is located in Northern Europe in a region called Scandinavia, made up of Norway and Denmark.

Norse Religion

The Swedes along with all other scandinavian tribes before and during the age of the viking followed the Norse religion. The Norse religion is polytheistic, there are two distinct groups of gods, Aesir and Vanir. The Aesir are chaotic and warlike, ruling over Asgard, while Vanir are peace-loving gods and are associated with fertility and nature, ruling over Vanaheimr. In the Norse faith, there exist nine worlds, Asgard, Alfheim, Midgard, Vanaheim, Jotunheim, Niflheim, Muspellsheim, Svartalfheim, and Helheim. All of these worlds are connected to one another through Yggdrasil, a cosmic tree of life that connects all of the worlds through its branches and roots. All living people are leaves of Yggdrasil, leaves that fall represent lives lost, while new budding leaves are new life that was born. The main god of the faith was Odin, he was called the All-Father because he was the father keen with knowledge and wisdom. He was also the ruler of Asgard. Odin saw in his prophecy Ragnarok, the end of the world, in a war between Aesir god against Hel and Loki, the trickster god.
